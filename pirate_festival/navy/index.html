Translation Notes

  1. ↑ Midori does this in other stories too, like Biblio.
  2. ↑ Uchiwa fans look like this, idol fans often customize their own uchiwa to bring to idol concerts, adding their a picture of their favorite idol’s face or putting words like “make a peace sign”, etc.
  3. ↑ Yuruchara; yuru meaning “loose,” “laid-back,” + the word “character”. Yuruchara are a type of mascot character, and their defining feature is their peculiar designs, ranging from bizarre-cute to adorkable. They’re well-known for being used all across Japan for PR purposes for companies, prefectures, etc… For more information read here.
  4. ↑ Kidoairaku (喜怒哀楽) is a 4-character idiom to mean “human emotions”, made up of the kanjis “joy”, “anger”, “sorrow”, “satisfaction”. Also, you can see how Captain Kidoairaka looks through Midori’s Buddy card.
  5. ↑ Referring to William Kid.
  6. ↑ Anego means “big sister”. It’s a respectful way to refer to a woman older than the speaker, or to refer to an elder sister.
  7. ↑ Also known as “wire fu”, it’s when stuntmen or actors utilize wires on their bodies to move around and perform action scenes.
