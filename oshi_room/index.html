Introduction

Welcome to the Room To Definitely Find A Favorite! Hosted by Hajime, he guides you to find the “oshi” (favorite) that best suits you. In here, you’ll find Ryuseitai’s answers to each step!

Fun Facts:

  • Kanata is the only character with neither an “Only Child” nor a “Sibling” tag. This is likely due to the ambiguity of Konata’s relations to Kanata, as it is not confirmed whether they’re related by blood or not.

  • Chiaki is a certified driver for motorcycles and cars. He’s had the motorcycle license since he was in high school, and received his car license in the early days of ES!! Era Year 2.

Keyword Notes:

  • Athletic Club and Culture Club is referring to their clubs in Yumenosaki.
  • “Mood Maker” refers to someone who brightens up the atmosphere and makes other people smile.
  • “Introverted” here could also be phrased as “Struggles to socialize with strangers”.
  • “Boke” and “Tsukkomi” are roles in a manzai comedy. The boke is the funny man who says nonsensical statements, and the tsukkomi is the straight man who retorts/corrects the boke’s comments. In this case, it’s likely referring to the position they typically end up in casual conversation, rather than an actual manzai comedy.

Chart Note: Advanced/Beginner is likely referring to how well they navigate an outside activity alone, while the left and right placements indicate how far they can do it alone.

The following is purely speculation, but as an example, Shinobu is shy in front of strangers, so he isn’t well-versed with navigating an activity alone in public. On the contrary, Chiaki is well-versed with how to approach a hero show as someone older than the general audience, but he can’t go that far alone as he usually prefers to spend time with others.

Translation Notes

  1. ↑ Radio calisthenics (ラジオ体操 lit., "radio exercises") are warm-up exercises performed to music and guidance from radio broadcasts.
  2. ↑ Chilled pineapple on a stick. It can look like this or like this.
  3. ↑ Umibouzu (海坊主) is a mythical sea youkai that breaks ships or drowns sailors.
  4. ↑ Referencing Tetora’s FS1 4* story, Midnight Encounter. You can find a translation on the masterlist.
  5. ↑ Abura soba is a soupless ramen dish, made of oil and soba (buckwheat noodles).
  6. ↑ Tetora is referring to this karate activity, done in the New Year’s.
  7. ↑ Chiaki is likely referring to the Hakone Ekiden, an annual long-distance relay right after the New Year’s. The athletes come from various universities in Japan.
  8. ↑ Kombu is kelp, used in osechi dishes (New Year’s food) as a wish for good health (in this case, longevity and joy). See more here.
  9. ↑ Hero shows are performance shows done live on a stage. The audience are typically kids (with their parents), and it’s encouraged to cheer for the hero on stage. As an extra fun fact; when Chiaki was still a student, he would perform hero shows in a park either by himself or with Ryuseitai.
  10. ↑ Chiaki’s referring to songs that appear in tokusatsu shows, especially the theme song played during a hero’s transformation.
  11. ↑ In karaoke, the lyrics are displayed through a video on a TV screen. The video contains images or footage related to the song.
