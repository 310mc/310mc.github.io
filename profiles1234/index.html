Story Notes

This is the first Ryuseitai story in the game, introducing the unit and its members to the readers.

The unit Ryuseitai is originally based on the “Super Sentai Series” tokusatsu featuring live-action superhero squads. Ryuseitai’s unit title during their Yumenosaki days is “The Sentai Hero unit that tolerates no evill!”. The English equivalent of their name would have been “Meteor Rangers”, hence their song, Tenka Muteki☆Meteorrangers!.

Super Sentai is intended for kids, and Ryuseitai also prides on performing for the sake of children. add more?

Ryuseitai’s members all have unique personalities, expressed in the way they speak or in their lifestyle. Here is a rundown of some notable traits:

Chiaki

Chiaki is typically referred to as the hot-blooded type (known as nekketsu-kei, 熱血系) in stories; people who are overly passionate and zealous about everything without forethought. These types of people tend to be called atsukurushii for being so enthusiastic that it can feel stuffy/suffocating. atsukurushii is usually translated with one of those words.

His first-person pronoun is ore (俺), a masculine pronoun.

Kanata

Kanata always talks in full hiragana, and he turns katakana words to deformed hiragana spelling. Hiragana-only dialogue is typically found in children’s games or nursery rhymes, as kids only know a few kanji at an early age (add when).

Japanese is difficult to parse without the use of kanji. This is because typical written Japanese doesn’t use spaces between words, and instead uses kanji to be able to tell where a word starts and ends. In the case of children games that are hiragana-only, they utilize spaces between words to make it easier for children to read. But in Enstars, Kanata’s dialogue instead uses brackets between words (that look like this 『』), which helps parse what he’s saying and give him a unique style of speaking.

Note: The only word Kanata always says in the correct way it’s spelt is “hero” (ヒーロー, in katakana).

He always speaks in polite speech (keigo), and his first-person pronoun is boku (ぼく), a pronoun typically used by guys.

Tetora

Tetora always speaks with the -ssu speech ender. This is a polite way of speaking, stemming from the keigo form of polite speech (masu/desu → shortened to “ssu”). This form of polite speech gives the impression of a martial artist character, as this is…. add more i’m getting lost in the sauce. He also uses typical phrases a martial artist character would use, like “Ossu!”.

Tetora is also typically referred to as taiikukai-kei in stories, usually written as “sportsy guy” in my translations. “Taiikukai-kei” is a word used to describe those who are part of a sports club. It’s also used to refer to a trope of that demographic, and they are typically associated with the following characteristics:

  1. “When there is a will, there is a way” belief
  2. Highly values a hierarchical system (ex. juniors respecting and obeying superiors)
  3. Highly values teamwork
  4. Highly values proper etiquette when greeting others

There are also more characteristics that are very fitting for Tetora’s character. Read more here (JP wiki).

His first-person pronoun is ore (俺), a masculine pronoun.

Midori

Midori's dialogue uses a lot of ellipses (…). He speaks informally with people his age, and switches to speaking more politely when addressing people older than him.

His first-person pronoun is ore (俺), a masculine pronoun.

Shinobu

Shinobu speaks in the typical speech quirk the ninja archetype uses in fictional stories.

  • “Sessha” is the first-person pronoun
  • “(de)gozaru” is the typical sentence-ender they use (roughly sounds like an old-fashioned, polite way of speaking)
  • “-dono” is a polite honorific, meaning “Mr.”, “lord”.

Sometimes Shinobu uses ninja-like terms in his speech, but he also mixes in modern lingo in, typically Japanese teen slang.
